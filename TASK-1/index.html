World War II

1939 – 1945 | The Deadliest Conflict in Human History

Explore Timeline

Introduction

World War II was fought between the Allies and Axis powers from 1939 to 1945. It involved over 100 countries, caused millions of deaths, and reshaped global politics with the Allied victory.

Causes of the War

  • Treaty of Versailles penalties on Germany
  • Rise of Fascism and Nazism
  • Expansionism by Germany, Italy, and Japan
  • Failure of appeasement policies

Major Events Timeline

1939: Germany invades Poland
1940: Fall of France, Battle of Britain
1941: Pearl Harbor, USSR invasion
1942–43: Stalingrad, El Alamein, Midway
1944: D-Day Normandy invasion
1945: Fall of Berlin, Hiroshima & Nagasaki

Consequences

The war caused over 60 million deaths, ended fascist regimes, exposed the horrors of the Holocaust, and led to the creation of the United Nations. It also marked the beginning of the Cold War between the USA and USSR.
